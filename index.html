DUI

What’s Happening: You’ve been taken to jail, probably saw a judge and likely posted bond. If your license was valid when you were arrested, your driving or business suspension only lasts for 10 days following your arrest. But now the clock is ticking. Action must be taken within 10 days of your arrest to allow you to keep driving.

Our Plan: I’ll work to challenge the suspension of your license or help you get a hardship license. If you are eligible to do this, we must act within 10 days of your arrest! If you miss this 10-day window, you can’t drive again for at least 30 to 90 days. Don’t wait a single day — call now! Once we’ve secured your ability to drive, we’ll examine the evidence and attack the criminal case against you.

DOMESTIC BATTERY

What’s Happening: Law enforcement responded to a report of a domestic incident involving you and a family member and you were arrested. You saw a judge and likely were told you can have no contact with the alleged victim, and in many cases, you were released from jail wondering what to do next.

Our Plan: Moving quickly is key in these situations. To begin prosecution, the State must file a document called an “Information.” I will immediately after your arrest and will work to have the case against you dismissed before an Information is filed. If a charge must be filed, I will work to make sure it is the lowest level offense possible. I will also file a motion asking the court to allow you to have contact with the alleged victim while the case is pending, provided they agree contact with you. If a charge has been filed, we will then explore your defenses and work quickly to bring about a favorable resolution.

DRUG OFFENSES

What’s Happening: You were arrested and accused of possessing narcotics. This could have happened in a variety of ways. You may have been arrested with drugs found on your person, you may have been stopped in a vehicle, or in some cases, a search warrant may have been executed on your home. A conviction for a drug-related felony or even a misdemeanor can affect your freedom, your future, and your ability to drive or earn a living.

Our Plan: I will first look at the circumstances of your arrest and challenge any unlawful searches. I will then reach out to the prosecutor to discuss any legal issues with the search and attempt to obtain a dismissal of the charges or a reduction to something less. This step is crucial in drug cases and having the right approach can shift the case from one where the State seeks punishment to one where rehabilitation becomes the focus, resulting in a favorable resolution that allows your life to go on and your future to remain intact.

BURGLARY

What’s Happening: You’ve been arrested after an incident during which you’ve been accused of entering a home, business, or vehicle unlawfully with the intent to commit a crime, like burglary or theft. You’re likely seeing a bond and may have been ordered to have no contact with the alleged victim. You may also have been told not to return to the location involved with the case. Depending on the charge, a burglary conviction can range from probation all the way to a life felony.

Our Plan: I will investigate the circumstances of your arrest and personally review all discovery materials. I will meet with the prosecutor to present the best findings. If no charges have been filed yet, I will work to prevent them from being filed. If charges must be filed, I will attempt to persuade the prosecutor to file the lowest allowable offense. I will then work tirelessly to achieve the best result possible as quickly as I can. I will review the evidence, interview witnesses, and leave no stone unturned while crafting an aggressive and comprehensive defense for your case.

If it’s alleged that the theft was committed with the use or threat of force, you are likely facing a robbery charge, which is an extremely serious felony that can result in a prison sentence.

PROBATION VIOLATIONS

What’s Happening: You were previously placed on probation and are now alleged to have violated one or more of the conditions. Violations can be “technical,” like failing to complete a class, evaluation, or community service, or failing a drug test. Violations resulting from an arrest for a new crime are known as “new-law” violations. Regardless of the type, all probation violations are serious because they typically result in a warrant being issued and persons sitting in jail for weeks or months waiting for their first court date.

Our Plan: If a warrant has not yet been issued or executed, I will immediately file a motion asking the court to schedule a hearing for an alternative resolution. If one has been issued or executed, whether you are in custody or scheduled for an in-custody violation hearing, I will immediately contact your probation officer to obtain the details of the alleged violation. Next, I will speak with the State to present your defenses or negotiate a favorable resolution. I will then appear at your hearing to either challenge the alleged violation or have you placed back on probation, if appropriate.

DRIVING OFFENSES

What’s Happening: You were pulled over and subsequently charged with a driving offense. These offenses can range from driving with a suspended license, to reckless driving, to fleeing and eluding, or even “Super Speeding” law, to racing on the highway. The consequences can be serious and may include jail time. In many driving offenses cases, there are technical or legal defenses that can help.

Our Plan: I will first review your driving record and then match it to the underlying facts of your situation. If you have been charged with racing, suspended license, or careless driving, I will examine whether the citation was lawfully issued and formulate any possible defenses. If your license is suspended, I’ll look for reinstatement options and explore whether they can be cleared so you can resume driving. The goal is always to get you driving legally again and keep you from having issues in the future.

EXPUNCTIONS & SEALING

What’s Happening: A charge against you has been dismissed or you had adjudication withheld and you may be eligible to have any case associated with your criminal history sealed or expunged. Doing so can remove any stigma from a charge or conviction.

Our Plan: We will meet and determine whether your record is eligible to be sealed or expunged. If eligible, we will obtain a certificate of eligibility from the State of Florida. Once we have the certificate, we’ll petition the court for expungement or sealing. If the plea is needed, I will prepare and file the pleading as needed to get your record sealed. This process can take up to 6 months, so don’t wait to get started.

If you are arrested...

Remain calm. Arguing with the officer or resisting the arrest can complicate the situation, and in some cases, lead to additional charges.

Request to speak with a lawyer and decline to answer any questions without counsel present.

Do not consent to any searches of your person, property or vehicle.

Do not discuss the facts or allegations of your case with anyone while you are in jail. NEVER DISCUSS THE FACTS OR ALLEGATIONS OF YOUR CASE ON A JAIL PHONE CALL. Jail calls are recorded and law enforcement officers and prosecutors can access your calls. If you discuss the details of your case you may inadvertently make admissions or give information that could later be used against you.

If you are eligible, post your bond as quickly as you can. Make sure you understand all conditions of your release, including any reporting requirements or “No Contact” orders.

Confirm your next court date.

Do not post anything about your arrest and the case on social media.
